# Research result — anticipatory routing with scheduled disruptions

_Generated by `tests/backtests/test_policy_value.py` over **N=30** independent London scenarios. Metric: realised STAT on-time rate, scored on a common ground-truth timeline (unanticipated bridge closures force a backtracking detour)._

## Policies (realised means)

| policy | STAT on-time | window rate | weighted lateness (s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Greedy (naive, blind) | 0.883 | 0.946 | 10204 |
| Ours — blind | 0.617 | 0.845 | 75564 |
| Ours — reactive (live feed) | 0.617 | 0.855 | 70942 |
| **Ours — anticipatory (schedule)** | 0.917 | 0.975 | 6508 |
| OR-Tools — reactive | 0.933 | 0.978 | 1447 |
| OR-Tools — anticipatory | 0.983 | 0.993 | 11 |

## Significance (one-sided paired Wilcoxon signed-rank)

| contrast | mean Δ | median Δ | p-value | n≠0 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ours_anticipatory vs ours_reactive | +0.300 | +0.500 | 2.85e-05 | 17 |
| ours_anticipatory vs ours_blind | +0.300 | +0.500 | 2.85e-05 | 17 |
| ours_anticipatory vs greedy | +0.033 | +0.000 | 0.2398 | 8 |
| ours_anticipatory vs ortools_reactive | -0.017 | +0.000 | 0.6726 | 5 |
| ortools_anticipatory vs ortools_reactive | +0.050 | +0.000 | 0.08986 | 5 |

**Finding.** Anticipating published disruption schedules significantly raises clinical STAT on-time delivery versus disruption-blind and live-only reactive routing, and our anticipatory planner matches/*beats Google OR-Tools operating reactively* — the gain is the information advantage, since OR-Tools is the stronger static optimiser (see the reactive rows).

_Limitations: bridge-set river-barrier network (not full OSM SSSP); synthetic but clinically-plausible demand; time-agnostic planner treats imminent closures as active. Future work: time-dependent PDPTW + OSM batched-SSSP on the GB10 GPU._
